MANAGING YOUR TIME THE RIGHT WAY

Wasting time in meetings is an important factor for most companies.In the last 20 years, the number of meetings has almost doubled, 67% of workers say spending too much time in meetings distracts them from doing their job. The cost of poorly organised meetings is $399 billion in the US in 2019 alone.

  • October 2020

  • Project Description

    TimePlanner is an application that manages meetings more efficiently. The application will be able to check if the respective meeting is needed and setting the level of need and if the persons involved are suitable for the meeting, using artificial intelligence based on the history of existing meetings. It will have a list of points to discuss for the respective meeting and the time allocated to each point, at the end there will be a feedback from the participants for the respective meeting which will be taken into account at the next meetings.

Interviews

During our phone interviews, we asked 6 relevant questions to regular software developers and 5 to those holding management positions. Depending on the availability of the interviewee, we either asked some additional questions or tried to get a more detailed answer. The questions are the following:

 

Regular employees:

  1. Personal information: age, experience, studies, role
  2. Company information: state owned/private, number of employees, Romanian/foreign, project team size
  3. Number of meetings per week
  4. Average duration of a team meeting
  5. Does your availability matters in scheduling meetings, especially in the context of work from home?
  6. Have you ever faced the situation where the meetings overlapped with each other or with other important activities?
  7. Did your feedback improve subsequent meetings ?
  8. What applications do you use for team communication and meetings and what do you think is the main problem with them ?
  9. Describe the experience of a meeting in one word.

 

Project managers:

  1. Company information: state owned/private, number of employees, Romanian/foreign, project team size
  2. Number of meetings per week
  3. Average duration of a team meeting
  4. How difficult is it to plan meetings so as to take into account the preferences of all team members (they might have calls, trainings,other problems) ?
  5. Do you meet objections when scheduling a meeting ? How often ?
  6. Do you take feedback into account? Is it something you still can't improve ?
  7. What applications do you use for team communication and meetings and what do you think is the main problem with them ?
  8. What is / was the main difficulty at the current job or at the last job?

5

What is the average duration of one meeting ?

It takes about half an hour.

Does your availability matters in scheduling meetings, especially in the context of work from home?

It matters because the Agile practices requires all of us to be present in order to discuss what we did yesterday and what we will work on today. This improves collaboration and synchronization of the team.

Have you faced the situation where the meetings overlapped with each other or with other important activities?

As our daily meeting is scheduled at 11 am, I avoid planning anything between 11 and 11:30. But sometimes, it may be postponed or canceled. I personally didn't have any problems when it happened, but there were some colleagues who couldn’t attend.

Did your feedback improve subsequent meetings ?

We give feedback at the end of each sprint. The project manager tries to improve the meetings as much as he can. Many things do depend on him.

What applications do you use for team communication and meetings and what do you think is the main problem with them ?

We use Microsoft Teams. It has some bugs: it sometimes blocks when sharing the screen, can’t detect the camera and need to be restarted.
